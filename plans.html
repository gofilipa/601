Outlining:

Review. 10m

Gather everything you have written so far about this paper; including analysis for source #1, freewrites from this class, and other notes or drafting you have undertaken outside of class.

Read through all these materials, one by one, and highlight any key ideas that you want to explore in your paper.

Brainstorm. 10m

Take out a blank sheet of paper or open a blank document.

Beyond what you've just reviewed in the previous step, write down all of the ideas that you want to discuss in your paper. Leave spaces between separate ideas, so you can distinguish between them.

There are no requirements for coherence or clarity. You may approach this as a kind of freewrite, where you just write what first comes to mind, while keeping separate ideas visually separated from one another on the page.

Outlining. 10m

Take out a blank sheet of paper or open a blank document.

From your highlights in the previous step, start to make a list of topics or ideas that you want to discuss in your paper.

2 things to keep in mind:

  • You will likely find some repetition, which is normal, but try not to repeat points. Either find some explicit difference or collapse the points into one.
  • You will likely need to break larger points down into smaller parts, to what you can say in one paragraph. Make sure you do so, but don't spend too much time getting too granular unless it's essential to your paper's research question.

Zoom in. 5m

Select 2 or 3 points that are the most important from the list of points you have just created. Highlight these points or this section of points. This is your main focus for the first draft! Everything else is either not a priority or will be contextual information.
